The future of hospitality design in the UK

In the rapidly changing landscape of hospitality in the UK, the expectations of travelers have evolved significantly and continue to do so. Beyond mere accommodation, today’s guests seek a homely atmosphere, a sense of community connection, and increased flexibility and personal control. Above all, they desire authenticity and attentive care. Hotels now face the challenge of providing a unified experience that encompasses all these elements.

Drawing inspiration from the UK’s diverse geography and rich cultural heritage, there is ample opportunity to design spaces that resonate authentically with their locations. This approach not only enriches the guest experience but also supports the UK’s aspiration to be a premier destination for immersive travel experiences.

The future of hospitality design in the Middle East

In Saudi Arabia’s city living vision for 2030, the focus for hospitality design is centered on human well-being and centricity. Nature, sustainability-inspired designs and community engagement promote mental and physical health, while technology enhances connectivity and convenience, enriching urban living experiences. Hotel design has also seen a shift from conventional space allocations to hybrid spaces that merges hospitality and residential living.
